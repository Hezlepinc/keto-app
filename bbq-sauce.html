Ingredients

  • 6 oz
    Tomato paste, no added sugar
  • 1/2 cup
    Water
  • 3 tbsp
    Apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp
    Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp
    Lakanto Golden Monk Fruitbrown sugar mimic; standard monk fruit works too
  • 1.5 tsp
    Smoked paprikathe smoke flavor; non-negotiable
  • 1 tsp
    Garlic powder
  • 1 tsp
    Onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp
    Salt
  • 1/2 tsp
    Black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp
    Ground mustard
  • 1/4 tsp
    Ground cinnamon (optional)depth + warmth
  • 1/4 tsp
    Liquid smoke (optional)amplifies the smoke flavor

Instructions

  1. Whisk all ingredients in a small saucepan over medium-low heat.
  2. Bring to a gentle simmer. Whisk occasionally for 10-15 minutes — longer simmer = deeper flavor. Don't boil hard.
  3. Taste at the 10-minute mark. Adjust: more Lakanto for sweeter, more smoked paprika for more BBQ punch, more ACV for tang.
  4. Cool completely. Pour into a mason jar with lid.
  5. Refrigerate. Keeps 3 weeks. Flavor deepens after 24 hours.
DIY Batch Prep Plan
Make alongside ketchup: same saucepan, same general method, just different spice profile. Knock both out in one 20-minute session.
Uses: brushing on chicken thighs the last 5 minutes of roasting, dip for keto pulled pork sandwiches (on chaffles), glaze for meatloaf, dip for wings.
Why this beats sugar-free store BBQ
Sugar-free BBQ sauces (G Hughes, Stubb's "Keto") use sucralose or maltitol. Even Primal Kitchen Classic BBQ uses balsamic vinegar and dates — fine for some but high carbs. This DIY version uses Lakanto Golden which gives that brown-sugar molasses note without any blood sugar impact, and the smoked paprika + liquid smoke combo recreates the cookout flavor.
