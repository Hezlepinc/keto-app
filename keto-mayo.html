Ingredients

  • 1 large
    Whole egg, room temperaturepasture-raised; room temp is critical for emulsion
  • 1 cup
    Avocado oilKirkland avocado oil; light olive oil also works (NOT EVOO — too bitter)
  • 1 tbsp
    Dijon mustardhelps emulsion stability; yellow works as backup
  • 1 tbsp
    Fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp
    Apple cider vinegarBragg's raw
  • 1/2 tsp
    Salt

Instructions (Immersion Blender Method)

  1. Use a tall, narrow jar that fits your immersion blender head exactly (a wide-mouth pint mason jar works perfectly).
  2. Crack the egg into the jar. Add Dijon, lemon juice, ACV, salt. Then pour avocado oil on top — it will float.
  3. Insert the immersion blender all the way to the bottom of the jar so the head is over the egg. Don't lift yet.
  4. Turn on full blast. Hold the blender at the bottom for 15-20 seconds — you'll see the mixture turn white and thick from the bottom up.
  5. Once the bottom half is fully emulsified, SLOWLY raise the blender (about 5 seconds to reach the top) while still running.
  6. Done. The mayo should be thick, pale yellow, glossy. Taste and adjust salt or lemon.
  7. Lid the jar. Refrigerate. Use within 1 week (raw egg).
DIY Batch Prep Plan
Sunday batch: double the recipe (2 eggs, 2 cups oil) → 2 mason jars. One jar for direct use, one becomes the base for the Ranch dressing recipe.
Replaces: Primal Kitchen Avocado Mayo. Used in pan-seared salmon dill cream, crispy thighs sauce, ranch dressing.
Why this beats store-bought
Standard mayo brands use soybean or canola oil — both inflammatory seed oils. Even "olive oil mayo" at the store typically blends 80% soybean oil + 20% olive. The DIY version is pure avocado oil + real egg + acid. Costs ~$2/jar vs $9 for Primal Kitchen.
Emulsion troubleshooting
If it breaks (looks like oily soup), don't toss. Crack a fresh egg into a new jar, blend it alone for 5 seconds, then SLOWLY drizzle in the broken mixture while blending. Saves the batch.
