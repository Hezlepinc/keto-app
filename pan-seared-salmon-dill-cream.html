Pan-Seared Salmon with Dill Cream

Instructions

  1. Whisk dill cream first: sour cream, mayo, dill, lemon juice, grated garlic, capers, salt and pepper. Refrigerate while you cook the salmon.
  2. Pat salmon completely dry with paper towels — this is the difference between crispy skin and rubbery skin. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat avocado oil in a stainless or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering (about 2 minutes).
  4. Place salmon skin-side DOWN. Press gently with a fish spatula for the first 20 seconds to prevent curling. Cook undisturbed 5-6 minutes.
  5. The salmon will release from the pan when the skin is properly crisp. Flip and cook flesh-side 2-3 minutes until internal temp reaches 125°F for medium (will carry to 130°F off heat).
  6. Transfer to plates skin-side up. Spoon dill cream alongside, not on top (keeps skin crisp).
  7. Serve with steamed asparagus in butter or arugula salad with EVOO and lemon.
Meal Plan Fit
Meal 1 (noon, post-WOD): 1 fillet + asparagus + 1/2 avocado = ~800 cal / 4g net carbs. Hits the omega-3 priority — Fatty fish 3-4x per week supports recovery and anti-inflammatory load.
Meal 3 (7pm): 1 fillet + side salad. Light, clean, low-insulin going into the overnight fast.
Why this is clean
Wild-caught salmon (avoid farmed when possible — higher omega-6, lower omega-3), avocado oil (NEVER vegetable oil for searing), real sour cream, avocado-oil-based mayo. The Primal Kitchen mayo swap is critical — standard mayo brands use soybean oil.
